## 4. Core thesis (3-5 предложений)

Yang-Lin тестируют, существует ли salary discrimination против international (foreign-born) NBA players. Using 1999-2008 panel, two-stage approach (stage 1 продуктивность; stage 2 salary), они находят, что international players receive ~10-15% lower salaries than US-born counterparts after controlling для productivity, position, height, draft pick, team market size — suggesting **nationality-based discrimination**. However, this discrimination effect varies by home-country market size: international players from large economies (China, Germany, Spain) receive higher salaries than international players from small economies — consistent с **home-country endorsement value** hypothesis. Authors also find U.S.-born white players paid less than U.S.-born Black players (suggesting reverse race effect, possibly via bench-player composition). **Implication:** salary determination в NBA reflects multidimensional discrimination + market-value effects, не pure productivity.

---

## 5. Key claims for our text (нумерованный список)

1. **International players paid 10-15% less** controlling for productivity (1999-2008 sample). **For us:** historical context — by our period (2015-2024), литература suggests this gap closed (Hill-Groothuis 2017 finds no discrimination); our INTL_BLOCK ≈ 0.5% consistent с this evolution.

2. **Home-country market matters:** international players from large economies (China, Germany) paid more than from small economies. **For us:** justifies our `continent` dummy approach (more nuanced than US-vs-international binary), но мы не have detailed home-country market size data; possible future extension.

3. **Two-stage methodology:** stage 1 estimates productivity, stage 2 salary on productivity + demographics; addresses endogeneity productivity-salary (если selection of foreign players biased). **For us:** standard methodological reference; our spec uses simpler single-stage.

4. **Race effects (U.S.-born):** white < Black (controlling productivity), authors interpret as bench-player composition или reverse discrimination. **For us:** не central, но Limitations consideration; we did not include race separately.

5. **Implication для talent allocation:** if international players paid less for same productivity, teams maximizing efficiency would over-select international (cheaper at margin). **For us:** historical context для why team scouts increasingly target international players (modern trend: Doncic, Antetokounmpo, Embiid all foreign-born); reflects increasing efficiency over time.

## 7. Methodological notes

- **Method name:** two-stage double fixed-effects salary regression
- **Key assumption:** stage 1 productivity estimation captures all relevant performance dimensions; player + year FE absorb time-invariant individual effects + macroeconomic salary growth
- **Where applicable in our work:** не directly применяется; наш single-stage spec с year FE + player observations more straightforward
- **Caveat:** two-stage может underestimate SE if stage 1 estimation noise not accounted for; modern bootstrap CI rec'd

## 8. Limitations / caveats

1. **1999-2008 sample** — historical; predates our post-2011 CBA era; institutional structure substantially different.
2. **Race effects controversial:** white < Black finding sensitive к sample period, control choices; modern literature reaches different conclusions.
3. **No formal causal identification** — discrimination interpretation rests on assumption all relevant productivity / market characteristics controlled.
4. **Continent / nationality classification** — paper uses simple US vs non-US binary plus a few country dummies; ignores within-international heterogeneity.
5. **Home-country market size measurement** — paper uses simple GDP proxy; more nuanced media-market measures unavailable.

## 9. Connection to our findings

**PARALLELS / HISTORICAL CONTEXT** для INTL_BLOCK в Shapley decomposition.

Прямое отображение:
- **Shapley INTL_BLOCK ≈ 0.5% (small)** — consistent с post-2010s literature trend, что nationality-based discrimination minimal в modern NBA (vs Yang-Lin 1999-2008 finding ~10-15% discount). Suggests temporal convergence; modern foreign players (Doncic, Embiid, Antetokounmpo) command top-tier max contracts comparable to US-born superstars
- **Sub-finding из Yang-Lin: home-country market effect** — possible refinement of наш `continent` dummies; e.g., European players from major economies (France, Germany, Spain) might command premium beyond simple European-vs-American dummy; future extension
- **H7 (anti-marketability):** Yang-Lin "team market size matters" partially parallels our H7 framework, но they look at home country (player origin), not team city; complementary dimensions
