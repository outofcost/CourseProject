## 4. Core thesis (3-5 предложений)

Hill-Groothuis analyze new NBA CBA (1999-end of lockout) и argue, что результатом was **"Robin Hood" rent redistribution** — surplus transferred from superstars (capped via max contracts) к median и lower-rank players (boosted via higher minimum salaries + cap-induced floor). Empirical evidence: Lorenz curve анализ first 2 seasons показывает все players below median wage gain (relative position); players near median gain most. Authors interpret через **median voter framework** — players' union effectively represents median player (largest voting block), and CBA designed to maximize median voter utility. **Implication:** institutional design results from political process within union, not pure market efficiency. Top-end max contracts and bottom-end minimum salaries — outcome of collective bargaining, не market signals MRP. **Connection to broader literature:** complement Rottenberg (1956) Coase / Invariance — Hill-Groothuis show explicit deviation from Coase due to collective bargaining institution.

## 5. Key claims for our text (нумерованный список)

1. **CBA 1999 introduced max contract cap:** players with > 6 years exp can earn max 30% of cap; veterans 10+ years 35%. **For us:** institutional foundation для tier classification — наш `classify_tier.py` directly implements analogue rules for current CBA.

2. **Lorenz curve redistribution evidence:** players below median wage all gain relative position под new CBA; near-median players gain most. **For us:** parallel наш H4 finding β_post_cba_2017 = +26% — but ours is OPPOSITE direction (top-tier gains via supermax), suggesting CBA 2017 reversed Robin Hood mechanism back to Sheriff-of-Nottingham (top-tier gain).

3. **Median voter model:** union representation favors median player → policies favoring median rather than top. **For us:** theoretical framework для discussing why CBA 2017 (designated extension / supermax) — political compromise between elite veterans (high voting power individually) and median players.

4. **Cap as economic constraint:** salary cap creates discrete tier structure (max, mid-level exception, MLE-tier, MLE-tier, veteran minimum). **For us:** institutional underpinning H3 finding R² = 0.85 на одних tier dummies.

5. **Empirical CBA analysis as methodology:** Lorenz curve + before-after comparison установлены Hill-Groothuis как standard approach для CBA impact studies. **For us:** Methods reference для our H4 event-study around CBA 2017.

## 8. Limitations / caveats

1. **2-season window** (1999-2001) — short post-CBA period; may capture transition dynamics, not long-run effects.
2. **No formal econometric regression** — descriptive Lorenz curves only; cannot isolate CBA effect from concurrent factors (TV deal renegotiation, expansion).
3. **Median voter model** — theoretical framework, not directly tested empirically в paper.
4. **No control for player heterogeneity** — Lorenz curve treats players as exchangeable; quality / experience confounders.
5. **Pre-2003 CBA** — institutional analysis specific to 1999 agreement; subsequent CBAs (2005, 2011, 2017, 2023) progressively modified rules.

## 9. Connection to our findings

**SUPPORTS / EXTENDS (institutional foundation для H3, H4).** Hill-Groothuis 2001 — единственный paper в нашем bibliography, который explicitly analyses NBA CBA impact through Lorenz/distribution lens; provides exact analytical framework для интерпретация наших institutional findings.

Прямое отображение:
- **H3 (R² tier_only = 0.85):** Hill-Groothuis prediction confirmed — institutional layer (cap + max contracts + tier rules) dominantly determines salary positions; individual performance secondary
- **H4 (β_post_cba_2017 = +26%):** Hill-Groothuis Robin Hood mechanism in REVERSE — CBA 2017 supermax provision creates **Sheriff of Nottingham** transfer (rent flows back to top-tier elite veterans). Our finding actually shows that institutional shift went opposite direction in 2017
- **Sub-finding aging-vet discount (multi_all_nba ≥3 → β = −0.22):** consistent with Hill-Groothuis observation that institutional structure favors median player; surviving veterans face institutional discount as team negotiating leverage shifts to median voters
- **H7 (anti-marketability):** Robin Hood / median-voter model — large-market teams may be constrained by collective bargaining outcomes, leading to apparent market discount
