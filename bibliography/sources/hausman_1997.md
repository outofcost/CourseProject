Также объясняет, почему awards lag effect (H6) большой — value capture требует new contract negotiation, не automatically internalized.

## 4. Core thesis (3-5 предложений)

Hausman & Leonard измеряют, что NBA superstars (focus on Jordan, Bird) generate massive revenue spillover на other teams через два канала: (1) TV ratings — игры с superstar получают на 30–50% more national ratings, (2) road-game attendance — visiting team attendance растёт на 20–50% когда superstar approaches. Authors estimate Michael Jordan's total externality value to other NBA teams ≈ $53 million annually (через national TV revenue share + paraphernalia + visiting gate). Это namesake fact "Jordan was worth $53M to other teams" — широко cited в sports econ literature. Authors then argue, что salary cap (NBA policy tool) — inefficient response to superstar externality: лучше использовать **tax on superstar salaries** (Pigovian-style), который redistributes externality value без distortion трейд markets and competitive balance. Empirically demonstrates через attendance regressions (Sections III–IV) и TV ratings analysis (Section IV).

## 5. Key claims for our text (нумерованный список)

1. **Direct attendance superstar effect:** Bird увеличил Celtics home attendance на ~50% (от 10K до 15K capacity, 1980-1989); Jordan удвоил Bulls home attendance в первый год (6K → 12K), затем full capacity (Section III.A, pp. 16–17). **Implication для NBA salary:** игрок-superstar генерирует team revenue × 1.5–2.0 свыше team-quality baseline.

2. **Road-game externality (cross-team):** 1989-1990 season, attendance Bulls road games sold out almost все (за исключением 1 game). Washington Bullets — attendance почти удвоилась когда Bulls visited (Table 1). Incremental away gate revenue из-за Bulls = $1.6M (1989-1990) и $2.5M (1991-1992) (Table 1-2). **For us:** даёт scale для market-size interactions в H7 spec'е — superstar в large markets generates ещё бОльшую spillover (TV reach + endorsement).

3. **TV ratings premium:** National OTA/cable ratings для games с Jordan / Bird на ~30–50% выше, controlling for team quality (Section IV, p. 19–22, не извлекаем точные beta из preview но reported в paper).

4. **Total Jordan externality ≈ $53M/year** (cited result, Section V): combined attendance + TV + paraphernalia + visiting gate. **For Discussion:** это classic supporting fact, что NBA salaries (даже max contracts $25M for top players) НЕ over-paid relative to MRP including externalities; в значительной степени under-paid.

5. **Salary cap = inefficient policy:** authors propose Pigovian tax on superstar salaries как efficient alternative; salary cap distorts allocation (Jordan worth more in Chicago than Sacramento, но cap forces redistribution). **Connection to H3 / H4:** наш finding tier dummies → R² = 0.85 means salary cap is dominant institutional layer; Hausman-Leonard 1997 предположил, что эта institutional layer inefficient.

## 6. Direct quote candidates (с page numbers)

> "We analyze the impact superstars have on attendance and television ratings and show that, in addition to the effect they have on their own teams, superstars exert a tremendous positive externality on other teams." (Abstract, p. 9)

> "Basketball is a sport well-suited for the development of superstars. Players are highly visible to fans because they do not wear headgear, only five players are on the floor at any one time, and fans are close to the action. In addition, although basketball is a team game, individual players can make spectacular individual plays. The advantage that NBA players have over players from other sports is demonstrated by the success NBA superstars have had in obtaining endorsements." (Introduction, p. 13)

> "Larry Bird had a substantial impact on the attendance of Celtics games at the Boston Garden after his entrance into the NBA … attendance increased by about 50% during the Bird era." (Section III.A, p. 17)

> "If we conservatively estimate the Jordan attendance effect by the increase of 6,000 per game in Jordan's first year and we assume that the incremental tickets were sold at the average ticket price, Jordan increased the Bulls' gate revenue by about $8.6 million." (Section III.A, p. 18)

> "We demonstrate that a salary cap is typically not the economically efficient way to achieve the goals of competitive balance and the financial viability of small market teams. Instead, we show that the league should impose a tax on superstar salaries. We show that the tax is less distortionary than the salary cap." (Abstract, p. 9)

## 8. Limitations / caveats

1. **Two-season window (1989-90, 1991-92):** small sample, limited generalizability beyond era; Jordan-Bird specific.
2. **Counterfactual problem:** estimating «what if Jordan never played» через before-and-after — confounded с general league growth, Stern's marketing, expansion teams (Hornets 1988, Magic 1989, T-Wolves & Heat 1989).
3. **Capacity constraints biased downwards** superstar effect — Stadium sold out for many superstar games; true demand under capacity unobserved.
4. **Endorsement valuation indirect:** authors use paraphernalia sales as proxy; endorsement income directly to player (e.g., $35M Jordan total earnings включая endorsements) not directly captured in team revenue.
5. **Salary cap policy recommendation untested:** Pigovian tax proposal — theoretical, NBA never implemented; we cannot empirically test claim that tax > cap.
6. **Doesn't control для team-specific time trends** rigorously — within-team variance в attendance может быть driven by other factors (new arena, ownership change, coaching).

## 9. Connection to our findings

**SUPPORTS / EXTENDS (theoretical and empirical foundation for awards channel).** Hausman-Leonard 1997 — единственный paper, который дает MRP-level magnitude superstar externality в NBA; помогает interpret наши findings о awards premium.

Прямое отображение:
- **H5 β_all_nba_lag1 = +0.185 (p = 0.008)** — All-NBA selection ≈ "superstar status"; salary premium consistent с magnitude Hausman-Leonard estimated externality ($53M/year for Jordan, ~17% of NBA TV revenue equally distributed)
- **H6 event study τ=+2, +3** — externality value capture требует contract renewal; player получает share только при negotiation, не automatically; объясняет delay
- **H7 anti-marketability β_top5 = −0.098** — если superstars в large markets создают бóльшую externality (TV reach, market size), почему salary discount? Hausman-Leonard offers альтернативное объяснение: large-market teams "captured" большую часть Jordan'ского externality сами (own-team revenue + central TV-revenue share), и могут negotiate ниже salary, поскольку другие team-revenue streams subsidize. Это complementary explanation Hembre-tax channel.

Для Discussion:
- "Hausman & Leonard (1997) estimated total cross-team externality from a NBA superstar at approximately $53 million annually; this provides the theoretical scale для interpretation наш awards finding"
- "В отличие от Hausman-Leonard sample (Bird/Jordan era, era pre-CBA-2017), our sample покрывает post-2011 CBA с разнообразным cap structure; awards effects сохраняются (H5/H6), но размер модерирован cap institutional constraint"

Для Introduction:
- "NBA — одна из немногих labor markets, где individual MRP может быть measured (через TV ratings, attendance), что мotivates NBA economics как laboratory (Kahn 2000); Hausman & Leonard (1997) дают canonical pre-cap estimate superstar value"

- **Adjacent NBA superstar literature:**
  - Berri & Schmidt (2006) "On the road with the National Basketball Association's superstar externality" *J Sports Econ* — updated estimates для post-Jordan era
  - Kaplan (2022) "Putting a price on popularity: Evidence from superstars in the NBA" *Economic Inquiry* — recent (LeBron era) externality estimates
  - Berri & Schmidt (2010) book *Stumbling on Wins* — broader synthesis
- В нашу bibliography эти могут быть additional_proposals (если direct Hausman-Leonard недостаточно для post-cap era).
- **Caveat для нашей курсовой:** Hausman-Leonard анализирует NBA до 1995 (one year of cap data only); current institutional layer существенно отличается. Findings о relative magnitudes (TV externality, road attendance premium) generalize, но specific numbers — нет.
- **Не путать с Hausman test (econometric)** — это same Jerry Hausman, но эта paper не про методологию.
