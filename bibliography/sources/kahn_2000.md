## 4. Core thesis (3-5 предложений)

Kahn argues, что professional sports labor markets дают unique testable evidence для economic theory due to (a) precise productivity measurement, (b) detailed compensation data, (c) clear institutional structure with periodic changes (CBAs) acting as natural experiments. Survey covers four major areas: (1) **monopsony rents** — pre-free-agency literature (Scully 1974, Sommers-Quinton 1982) показывает что players paid below MRP under reserve clause, supports Rottenberg's argument; (2) **discrimination** — early work (Kahn-Sherer 1988, Brown-Spiro-Keenan 1991) finds modest racial salary differentials in NBA, controversial empirical methods; (3) **Coase theorem** — talent allocation pre/post free agency tested, finds mixed evidence (some redistribution); (4) **incentive contracts** — bonus structures, contract-year effects, manager succession. Conclusion: sports data «punch above their weight» в economics — small industry empirically, large impact on labor methodology.

## 5. Key claims for our text (нумерованный список)

1. **Sports as ideal labor market lab:** Kahn argues productivity, compensation, и institutional structure all observable in sports — rare combination in any labor market. **For our Introduction:** обязательная mотивация выбора NBA.

2. **Pre-free-agency monopsony:** Scully (1974) и others find players paid 10-30% below estimated MRP под reserve clause; post-free-agency salaries rise toward MRP. **For us:** historical context для современного NBA, где CBA + cap restrict но не fully suppress wages.

3. **Discrimination findings (controversial):** Kahn-Sherer (1988) finds 20-25% lower salaries for Black NBA players, controlling for measured performance — but later work suggests this may reflect measurement issues, not pure discrimination. **For us:** justify excluding race directly from main spec (we use born_country и continent dummies instead — closer to "international" dimension); race-related findings in our work treat as Limitations.

4. **Coase theorem evidence:** Daly-Moore (1981) and others test allocation efficiency before/after free agency — find some support but not complete Coase efficiency. **For us:** parallel к H7/H8 — strong team / market effects suggest Coase efficiency partially broken.

5. **Incentive / contract literature:** Berri et al., Stiroh (2007), Maxcy-Fort-Krautmann — strong evidence для contract-year effect, MRP responsiveness. **For us:** justifies inclusion lagged variables и awards features в our spec.

## 8. Limitations / caveats

1. **Survey through 1999** — pre-modern NBA institutional layer (post-1999 lockout, 2011 CBA, 2017 designated extension). Updates needed for current institutional context.
2. **No new empirical results** — review article only.
3. **Coverage biased toward MLB** — most early sports econ literature was baseball-based; NBA literature thinner pre-1999.
4. **No methodological innovations claimed** — purely synthesis.
