## 4. Core thesis (3-5 предложений)

Rottenberg анализирует MLB labor market в эпоху reserve clause (1956 era) и формулирует, что reserve clause НЕ обеспечивает competitive balance — он лишь redistribute surplus от players к owners. **Invariance Principle:** под competitive market, allocation player talent across teams will be the same под free agency, как под reserve clause; рынок sorts игроков на highest-MRP teams независимо от property rights. Reserve clause therefore inefficient — creates monopsony power для teams, depresses player wages, без achieving stated goal balance. Rottenberg argues for free agency: efficiency dictates wages = MRP, не administrative caps. **Это foundational restatement Coase theorem в sports labor context** — preceded Coase (1960) на 4 года! Empirical observations включают reasoning, что cities имеющие большие fan bases generate higher revenue → MRP высший, поэтому they would still bid up top talent under free agency.

## 5. Key claims for our text (нумерованный список)

1. **Invariance Principle (Coase-style):** "the distribution of playing talent among teams in the league does not depend on the structure of the labor market in the sport" (paraphrased). Under both reserve clause and free agency, talent flows to teams where MRP highest. **For us:** suggests H7/H8 should find null или small effects after controlling for player productivity — что мы observed (H8 team controls null p>0.15).

2. **Reserve clause = monopsony, not balance tool:** restricting player mobility transfers surplus to owners without changing talent allocation. **For us:** institutional motivation для studying salary cap effects (H3/H4) — modern cap may have similar surplus-transfer effect.

3. **Cities with large fan bases generate higher MRP:** Rottenberg observes (descriptive only): large-market teams have higher revenue, so they would still acquire top talent regardless. **For us:** explains why H7 finding β_top5 = −0.098 is surprising — Rottenberg framework predicts large markets should pay PREMIUM. Our anti-marketability finding contradicts naïve Rottenberg expectation; resolution через either Hembre tax channel или Hausman-Leonard endorsement-substitution channel.

4. **Free agency would increase efficiency без damaging balance:** Rottenberg's prediction confirmed historically when MLB and other leagues introduced free agency — competitive balance не changed significantly, but player wages rose to MRP. **For us:** parallel to NBA's post-2011 institutional changes — designated extension and supermax are «monitored free agency» variants.

5. **First paper in sports econ literature:** widely acknowledged как founding contribution; cited везде в NBA / NFL / MLB economics. Required reading для Introduction / first chapter.

## 8. Limitations / caveats

1. **No formal model — verbal analysis only.** No empirical estimates of magnitude monopsony rent transfer.
2. **MLB-specific institutional analysis** — NBA institutional layer (salary cap, max contracts, designated extension) более complex; Rottenberg framework не directly transposable, requires modifications.
3. **Pre-data era** — нет detailed contract data в 1956; analysis qualitative.
4. **Invariance Principle assumes zero transaction costs** — under cap binding (как в NBA), transaction costs create wedge; Hausman-Leonard (1997) уже argued Coase ne holds в NBA due to cap.
5. **Анализ только labor market** — не addresses fan welfare, league revenue maximization, owner welfare separately.

## 9. Connection to our findings

**PARALLELS / EXTENDS (historical foundation для interpretation team/market effects).** Rottenberg's Invariance Principle relevant to interpretation наших H7 и H8 findings:

- **H8 (team controls null, p > 0.15):** Rottenberg-Coase predicts что team-level success metrics (win%, playoffs, luxury tax) не должны explain individual salary variance после controlling player productivity, если Coase holds. Our null finding ✅ consistent с Rottenberg framework — talent allocates to where MRP highest, but salary тоже reflects MRP, не team success
- **H7 (anti-marketability β_top5 = −0.098):** Rottenberg simple version expects large-market premium (higher MRP); our reverse finding (discount) suggests Coase-style efficiency broken в modern NBA через institutional constraints (salary cap binding для top teams), OR через compensating differentials (Hembre tax channel)
- **H3/H4 (institutional layer dominant, R² tier-only = 0.85):** Rottenberg's critique of reserve clause as wealth transfer mechanism — modern analogue is NBA cap structure, which transfers surplus from top players to median и owners (consistent c Hill-Groothuis 2001 "Robin Hood" framework)

Для Introduction:
- "С Rottenberg (1956), который положил начало sports economics literature, anaylses NBA salaries опираются на framework labor market efficiency, restricted by collective bargaining institutional layer"

Для Lit Review (brief context paragraph):
- "The literature on sports labor markets originates with Rottenberg (1956), who formulated an early Coase-style Invariance Principle showing that talent allocation is largely independent of institutional restrictions; subsequent decades of empirical work (Kahn 2000; Krautmann 1999; Berri et al. 2007) have tested и refined this framework, finding numerous deviations from strict Coase efficiency."
